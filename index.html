About

This tracker displays weighted estimates of NYC mayoral approval (Mamdani) based on daily online survey data collected by Nira Data.

Approve % and Disapprove % use total respondents as denominator (including “Don’t know”). Error bars / shaded ribbons show 95% confidence intervals.

The Latest (3-day) tab uses a rolling 3-day window; the Stable (7-day) tab appears once 7+ days of data are collected. The Extended (30-day) tab provides the most stable estimate using a wider rolling window (requires 10+ distinct days of data).

Overview

This tracker displays weighted estimates of NYC mayoral approval based on daily online survey data. All three estimates are weighted to match NYC adult population demographics using iterative proportional fitting (raking). Confidence intervals are 95%. Approval is reported using the Gallup convention: “Don’t know” responses are included in the denominator, so Approve% + Disapprove% + DK% = 100%.

The Latest estimate uses Lite Weighting with fewer categories to accommodate smaller sample sizes:

  • Age (3 categories: 18-34, 35-54, 55+)
  • Gender (2 categories: Male, Female)
  • Borough (5 categories)
  • Race/Ethnicity (4 categories: White, Black, Hispanic, Asian/Other)
  • 2025 Mayoral Vote (4 categories: Mamdani, Cuomo, Other candidate, No vote)

The Stable and Extended estimates use Standard Weighting with the following variables:

  • Age × Gender (joint distribution, 12 cells)
  • Borough (5 categories: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island)
  • Race/Ethnicity (4 categories: White, Black, Hispanic, Asian/Other)
  • Education (4 categories: HS or less, Some college, Bachelor’s, Graduate+)
  • 2025 Mayoral Vote (4 categories: Mamdani, Cuomo, Other candidate, No vote)
  • Vote × Borough (joint, 20 cells — when sample size permits)
Additional Notes
  • Population targets are derived from the American Community Survey (ACS) 2023 5-year estimates and official NYC Board of Elections results.
  • Weight trimming is applied to prevent any single respondent from having excessive influence (weights capped at 6× the mean).
  • Weight normalization: weights are normalized to mean = 1.
  • Design effect measures the loss of precision due to weighting; values typically range from 1.2 to 3.0.
  • Data collection is conducted daily via online panel by Nira Data.
